Historical Timeline of Laws

1942: In 1942, India was still under British colonial rule. The struggle for independence was at its peak, and legal developments were intertwined with the broader political context. The British administration had implemented various laws, including the Criminal Tribes Act of 1871, which stigmatized certain communities. The legal framework at this time laid the groundwork for post-independence reforms. 1951: In 1951, the first census was conducted post-independence, which included the identification of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. 1992:In 1992, one of the significant developments was the implementation of the 73rd Amendment to the Constitution, which decentralized power to the local level through Panchayati Raj institutions. This had implications for marginalized communities as it aimed at ensuring their participation in grassroots governance. 2019:In 2019, the legal landscape witnessed discussions and debates around the constitutional validity of affirmative action policies, particularly with respect to reservations in education and employment.
